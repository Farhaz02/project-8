History of

Valentine

Saint Valentine (Italian: San Valentino, Latin: Valentinus), officially Saint Vaine of Terni, is a widely recognized third-century Roman saint commemorated on February 14 and since the High Middle Ages is associated with a tradition of courtly love.

All that is reliably known of the saint commemorated on February 14 is his name and that he was martyred and buried at a cemetery on the Via Flamiia close to the Ponte Milvio to the north of Rome on that day. A gift or a present is an item given to someone without the expectation of payment or return. An item is not a gift, if that item, itself, is already owned by the one to whom it is given. Although gift-giving might involve an expeion of reciprocity, a gift is meant to be free.
